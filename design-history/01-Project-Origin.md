# Project Origin

## Background

CampusConnect did not start as a complete college management platform. The initial idea was much smaller and focused on solving a practical problem faced by student clubs while organizing events.

The first version of the project was called **Club Manager**.

## The Problem

While organizing events, clubs typically relied on WhatsApp groups, emails, and Google Forms.

This created several challenges:

- Event announcements were shared across multiple WhatsApp groups and email threads, causing important updates to be missed.
- Club members had to visit classrooms personally to inform students about events.
- Registration was handled through Google Forms, requiring students to repeatedly enter the same personal information for every event.
- Club organizers had to manually manage Google Form responses and prepare participant lists.
- Students often missed events from clubs they were interested in because there was no subscription mechanism.

Overall, the event management process was repetitive, manual, and inefficient for both students and organizers.

## Initial Solution

The idea behind Club Manager was to build a centralized platform connecting clubs and students.

The primary features included:

- Student registration with verified college information.
- One-click event registration using previously stored student details.
- One-click event unregistration.
- Club-based announcements.
- Club subscription system.
- Priority notifications for subscribed clubs.
- Exportable event registrations for organizers.

The objective was to eliminate repetitive Google Forms and replace scattered communication channels with a dedicated platform.

## Evolution into CampusConnect

While designing Club Manager, it became clear that colleges required more than event management.

Additional academic and campus-wide modules were introduced, including:

- Digital Campus Newspaper
- Research Paper Submission & Publishing
- Student Journalists
- Professor-based Review Process
- College-wide Announcements

As the scope expanded beyond clubs, the project was renamed to **CampusConnect**.

## Original Vision

The original vision was to provide a unified digital ecosystem for a **single college**, where students, clubs, professors, and administrators could interact through one platform instead of relying on multiple disconnected tools.
